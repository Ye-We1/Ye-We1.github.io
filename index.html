Research

During my PhD, my research focuses on deep learning techniques for processing and analysing sequential and structural data. I explore methods for effective representation learning from these data types, aiming to enhance risk prediction, uncertainty analysis, and future forecasting. This includes various approaches such as large-scale data mining, uncertainty estimation, noisy label learning, and domain generalisation, with applications in software layer (traffic forecasting) and hardware layer (neuromorphic computing).

Memory-facilitated Joint-space Shift Adaptation in Traffic Forecasting
Ye Wei, Haitao He, Gerald Schaefer, Zhigang Ji, Yifan Wang, Hui Fang
International Joint Conference on Neural Networks (IJCNN), 2024

We propose a memory-facilitated joint-space shift adaptation framework to address the distribution shift problem in traffic forecasting.
