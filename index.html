Research

My PhD research focuses on deep learning techniques for processing and analysing sequential and structural data. I explore methods for effective representation learning from these data types, aiming to enhance risk prediction, uncertainty analysis, and future forecasting. This includes various approaches such as large-scale data mining, uncertainty estimation, noisy label learning, and domain generalisation, with applications in software layer (traffic forecasting) and hardware layer (neuromorphic computing).
